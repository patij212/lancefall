# LANCEFALL — in plain English

*A neon game about bringing light back to a city that was made to forget itself. Free to play in your browser — I built it in two weeks for a game contest celebrating Alan Turing.*

## The story

There was once a city made of light, where the people kept everything they loved — their memories, their history, even their names — written in light itself.

Then it fell. And the ones who let it fall did something crueller than just leaving it in ruins: they **scrambled the whole city into grey static**, so that no one could even remember what had been lost. You can't mourn what you can't recall.

You are the last one who can undo it — a lone spear of light. Every fight you win and every code you crack brings a little colour back to the grey. Do it enough, and the sun finally rises over the city again: **the longest day of the year, when the light wins.**

## What you actually do

Here's the hook: **you don't have a gun.**

Your only move is a **dash** — a quick burst forward. But while you're dashing you *become* a spear of light: nothing can touch you, and you cut down everything in your path. So the game is all about *timing*. You watch the storm of bullets coming at you, wait for the right moment, and dash **straight through it** — skewering a whole line of enemies and coming out clean on the other side. Dodging and attacking are the same move. It feels great when it clicks.

And the bosses are **locked** — you can't just hit them. Each one hides behind a little **code**, and the game gives you the clue to crack it. You read the clue, work out the answer, and "punch it in" by dashing to the right spots — all while still dodging everything flying at you. It's like cracking a safe in the middle of a firefight. Solve it and the boss breaks open. Solve the very last one, and the whole world floods back into colour and daylight.

That's the heart of it: **dash, dodge, crack the code, bring back the light.**

## Why it's a tribute to Alan Turing

The contest I made this for honours **Alan Turing** — the man who cracked Nazi codes in World War II and, along the way, helped invent the computer. So it felt right to build a game where **breaking codes is the whole point.**

A few of his famous ideas are tucked inside, turned into things you actually do:

- One boss **copies your every move** and dares you to say which of you is the real one. *(Turing's most famous question was whether you could even tell a machine apart from a person.)*
- The very ending gives you a **choice no computer could make for you** — you simply have to decide, yourself.
- And the game plays out **exactly the same for every player**, like a machine following precise rules.

You don't need to know any of that to enjoy it. It's just there if you go looking.

## The honest bit

I built this **on my own, from nothing, in about two weeks**, just for the jam — no game engine, no team, just code. It runs right in your browser, it's completely free, and there's nothing to install.

▶️ **Play it here: https://lancefall.pages.dev**

One tip: dash *through* the bullets, not around them. Once that feeling clicks, you've got the whole game.
